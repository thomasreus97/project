Forest & Nature

Forest & Nature consists of forest and open natural areas. Forest is planted area with trees for wood production and/or nature management. This included unpaved and semi-paved roads from 1996 onward. Open natural areas are terrain in a dry and wet natural state and that including dikes without roads and natural pools up to and including 1959.

Recreation

Recreation areas are meant for entertainment and include parks, public gardens and sports grounds. Public gardens are landscapes in use for relaxation and other recreational areas are sites that are used as an allotment garden, for day recreation or residential recreation.

Agricultural

Agriculture is an terrain for the use of agriculture farming and crop production. A special form is greenhouse horticulture that can control abiotic and biotic factors. Between 1899 and 1939, cultivated land consisted of gardens for their own use and from 1940 to 1945 this was not taken into account. From 1946 to 1976, cultivated land consisted of, among other things, narrow ditches, unpaved roads and gardens for their own use, while these factors were again excluded from 1977 onwards. From 1996, where factors such as unpaved and semi-paved roads included in the definition of cultivated land.

Semi-build

The category of semi-built terrain consists of mineral extraction site, building site and other semi-built sites. Mineral extraction sites are areas for extracting raw materials from the soil and from 1996 that excluded water.

Traffic

The terrain used for transport is defined as an area in use for transport by rail, road and air. An area that is used for train tracks and transport by rails up to 1993 also includes areas for trams and metros. Road traffic areas are now only main roads in use for transport but up to and including 1993, unpaved roads were also included in this category. The airport is intended for the use of transport by air.

Build

Building sides are comprised out of three categories which are residential area, business sides and socio-cultural facilities. Residential areas are terrains in use for housing and highly related activities. Business sides before 1976 are only comprised of Industrial area, while after that the building area exists out of numbers elements such as catering industry, public facilities and merged company premises.

Water

Water consists of inland waters and outside waters. Inland water such as rivers, canals and lakes is water broader than six meters that is not in open communication with the sea. Outside water is water that is in open communication with the North Sea. Various examples of outside waters in the Netherlands are the Wadden Sea, Ooster- and Westerschelde. The "Nieuwe Waterweg" is closed by an imaginary line between the harbor heads.

Undefined

Undefined is the part of the total area which is not defined by all occupancies combined.
